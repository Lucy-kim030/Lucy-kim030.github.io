What is this study about?

Our team is developing wearable haptic wristbands that help individuals with low vision navigate complex environments. In this VR experiment, you will experience simulated prosthetic vision—a digital representation of what a bionic eye user might see.

By participating, you provide vital data on how humans process tactile information alongside limited visual cues. This research directly informs the design of next-generation assistive devices.

Impact

Real-world aid for visual impairments

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need VR experience? expand_more
No prior experience is required! We provide a full orientation and practice time during Session 1 to ensure you are comfortable with the equipment and the virtual environment.
Will I feel sick? expand_more
While motion sickness is a small possibility in VR, we utilize high-refresh-rate headsets and design our simulations to minimize discomfort. We specifically screen for a history of motion sickness to protect our participants.
What should I bring? expand_more
Just yourself! If you wear glasses or contact lenses, please bring/wear them. We recommend wearing comfortable flat shoes as you will be walking short distances.
Can I do both sessions on the same day? expand_more
No, we require sessions to be held on different days (at least 24 hours apart). This is to ensure data accuracy and to prevent participant fatigue or "VR hangover."
How do I get paid? expand_more
Reimbursement ($50 per session) is typically processed via bank transfer or gift card upon completion of each session. You will receive the details during your first visit.
